The School of Greatness

The School of Greatness is one of my favorite podcasts and is a huge inspiration for My Heroic Life. Lewis was a former NFL athlete who’s career ended early due to injury. He turned his life around and created one of the most successful podcasts in the world. He has recordedhundredsof inspirational interviews with celebrities, gurus and entrepreneurs who all have a unique perspective on greatness, personal development, health and so much more.

www.lewishowes.com

The One You Feed

“The One You Feed podcast uses the parable of two wolves at battle within us as a jumping-off point for meaningful discussions about how to live skillfully amidst life’s inherent challenges.”

I highly recommend this podcast. There were so many times in my life where I was facing a difficulty and turned to one the episodes from The One You Feed. There are so many deep meaningful discussions on life and how we react to inner challenges.

Website: www.oneyoufeed.net

Good Life Project

The Good Life Project is a motivational podcast that interviews people week after week who have risen above it all and share truly inspiring stories about hardships that they have overcome.

“Every week, we share inspirational, intimate and disarmingly-unfiltered conversations about living a fully-engaged, fiercely-connected and purpose-drenched life.”

Website:www.goodlifeproject.com/podcast

The Minimalists

This podcast is all about living with less. Living a more purposeful and more meaningful life with less clutter. The episodes are simple, motivational and straightforward.

Website: www.theminimalists.com/

How I Built This

I am a big fan of How I Built This. Every week Guy Raz interviews someone who built a business or company from the ground up. “How I Built This weaves a narrative journey about innovators, entrepreneurs and idealists—and the movements they built.”

Website: www.theminimalists.com/

Lewis Howes: School of Greatness

Lewis Howes’ book is similar to his podcast, but goes deeper into his personal life story, how he overcame setbacks and climbed to his success. In one of my favorite sections in this book, Lewis shares an inspiring story about his brother’s success, and dives into the nature of working hard to achieve our goals. This book taught me so much.

Can’t Hurt Me: David Goggins

Can’t Hurt me was a complete game changer for me. You think you’ve had it vad? Read this mans story. David Goggins suffered one of the most horrific childhoods imaginable, fell into a deep depression and weighed nearly 300 pounds in his early twentys. He decided to change his life and studied and trained to become a Navy Seal. He lost the weight needed in a very short time and achieved his goal. Goggins faced setback after setback in his life. I cannot put into words how motivational this book is!

The Power of Habit: Charles Duhigg

The Power of Habit is a fantastic study on how we develop habits and become the people we hope to be. In my own life I saw massive changes by starting very, very small. If nothing else, the prologue in this book shares an incredible story about a woman who completely changed in just a few years by changing small habits.

The Subtle Art of Not giving a F*ck: Mark Manson

The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F* is a fun and motivational read. Mark Manson just has a way of calling out your BS in ways your friends and family members won’t. We all hold onto things in life, and Mark’s approach to happiness is very simple: just care less about the things that don’t matter, and start giving a F* about the things that do.

Waking up – Sam Harris

This is my primary meditation app. Sam Harris is a renowned neuroscientist and philosopher, and I find his insights into meditation to be very helpful. He has an incredible database of meditation courses that I’ve found to be very purposeful and I feel like I make progress every time I use it.

Insight Timer

Insight Timer is massive library for mediation, relaxation, sleep, and more. If you’re looking for a meditation app with hundreds of different subjects and guided help from a multitude of leaders, this is by far the most helpful meditation app. Their free library alone is massive.

Headspace

If you’ve thought about meditating, odds are you’ve probably heard of headspace. They are arguably the most popular meditation app and offer guided meditations for stress, anxiety, work, kids, sleep and focus.
